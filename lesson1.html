‚ôø Assistive Technologies

Tools or devices designed to help people with disabilities perform tasks more easily and independently.

üëÅÔ∏è Vision Impairment

Partial or complete loss of sight affecting digital interaction

  • Screen Readers (JAWS, NVDA): Reads text aloud
  • Magnification Software: Zooms content for easier viewing
  • Braille Displays: Converts text to tactile braille
  • Audio Description: Explains visual elements in videos

üëÇ Hearing Impairment

Partial or complete loss of hearing affecting communication

  • Hearing Aids: Amplifies sounds
  • Cochlear Implants: Electronic devices for clearer hearing
  • Captioning Services: Adds subtitles to videos
  • Sign Language Apps: Translates to sign language

ü¶æ Mobility Impairment

Affects ability to move or use hands efficiently

  • Voice Recognition (Dragon): Voice-controlled computing
  • Eye-Tracking Technology: Control devices with eye movement
  • Adaptive Keyboards: Large keys or one-handed layouts
  • Switch Access: Single-switch device control

üß© Cognitive/Learning Disabilities

Affects thinking, learning, or information processing

  • Text-to-Speech: Reads text aloud for dyslexia support
  • Speech-to-Text: Converts spoken words to written text
  • Organizational Apps: Reminders and task managers
  • Simplified Interfaces: Easier navigation systems
