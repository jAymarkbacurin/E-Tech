üõ°Ô∏è 1. Online Safety ‚Äî Protecting Yourself from Harm

Online safety means taking steps to protect yourself, your personal information, and your well-being while using the internet. It involves recognizing risks and knowing how to respond to online threats.

‚ö†Ô∏è Common Online Threats

üò¢ Cyberbullying

When someone harasses, threatens, or humiliates another person online. It can cause emotional distress, anxiety, or depression.

üõ°Ô∏è What to do: Block the person, report the behavior, and tell a trusted adult immediately.

üë§ Online Predators

Adults who try to exploit minors for harmful purposes online.

üö© Red Flags:
  • Asks for personal information
  • Wants to meet alone
  • Sends inappropriate content

üé£ Scams and Phishing

Tricks used to steal your money or personal information through fake messages or websites.

Stay Safe: Always verify senders, avoid suspicious links, and check URLs carefully.

üÜî Identity Theft

When someone steals your personal information to commit fraud.

Protection: Don't overshare online and use privacy settings on all accounts.

‚õî Inappropriate Content

Material that is violent, sexual, or hateful.

Safety Tips: Avoid risky websites, use parental controls, and report harmful content.

‚úÖ Safety Tips to Remember

  • Use privacy settings on all social media
  • Think before you post ‚Äî it's permanent
  • Trust your instincts ‚Äî if something feels wrong, it probably is
  • Keep personal information private (address, phone number, location)
  • Tell someone if you feel unsafe online

üîê 2. Online Security ‚Äî Protecting Your Data and Devices

Online security means keeping your information and devices safe from hackers or cyberattacks. It includes using strong passwords, secure networks, and protective tools.

üîë Strong Passwords

Creating secure passwords is your first line of defense against unauthorized access.

Password Requirements:

  • At least 12 characters long
  • Mix uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols
  • Avoid dictionary words, birthdays, or "password123"
  • Use different passwords for different accounts
  • Consider a password manager (LastPass, 1Password)

‚öñÔ∏è 3. Online Ethics ‚Äî Doing the Right Thing

Online ethics involves making responsible choices that respect others and follow laws and moral principles in digital spaces.

üö´ Cyberbullying is Never OK

Don't send hate messages, spread rumors, or exclude people online. Stand up for victims and report bullying behavior immediately.

üè¥‚Äç‚ò†Ô∏è Digital Piracy

Illegally downloading or sharing copyrighted content (movies, music, software) without permission.

Better Alternative: Use legal streaming services like Netflix, Spotify, or Apple Music to support creators and avoid malware risks.

üìù Plagiarism and Intellectual Property

Using someone's work without giving proper credit is wrong and can have serious academic or legal consequences.

  • Always cite your sources properly
  • Paraphrase content and give attribution
  • Use plagiarism checkers before submitting work
  • Respect copyright and creative ownership

üîê Privacy and Consent

Don't share someone's private information, photos, or messages without their explicit permission. Respect digital boundaries just as you would in person.

Never Share Without Permission

‚úÖ Fact-Checking and Misinformation

In the age of viral content, it's crucial to verify information before sharing.

  • Check multiple reliable sources
  • Look for original sources, not just social media posts
  • Avoid spreading unverified rumors or conspiracy theories
  • Use fact-checking websites when in doubt

üìú Republic Act No. 10175 ‚Äî Cybercrime Prevention Act of 2012

Protects individuals and organizations from cybercrimes such as hacking, identity theft, cyberbullying, and online fraud.

  • Punishments include fines and imprisonment depending on severity
  • Covers computer-related fraud and cybersex
  • Addresses illegal access to computer systems
  • Includes libel committed online
Legal Protection

üîí Republic Act No. 10173 ‚Äî Data Privacy Act of 2012

Protects personal information stored in digital or physical form from misuse, unauthorized access, or disclosure.

  • Requires companies to implement data security measures
  • Organizations must obtain consent before collecting personal info
  • Gives individuals the right to know what data is collected
  • Allows people to request correction or deletion of their data
